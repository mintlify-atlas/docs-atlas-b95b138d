## Sign up for ScholarXIV

Get started with ScholarXIV by creating your account. The platform supports multiple authentication methods for your convenience.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the sign-in page">
    Navigate to [scholarxiv.com](https://scholarxiv.com) and you'll be directed to the authentication page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your authentication method">
    Select from multiple sign-in options:

    - **Google** - Sign in with your Google account
    - **GitHub** - Authenticate using your GitHub credentials
    - **Twitter** - Use your Twitter/X account
    - **Hugging Face** - Sign in with your Hugging Face account

    <Note>
      All authentication methods provide the same access to ScholarXIV features. Choose the provider that's most convenient for you.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a subscription plan">
    After authentication, you'll be directed to choose your subscription tier:

    - **Free** - Essential features for getting started
    - **Plus** - Enhanced limits and features for active researchers
    - **Pro** - Unlimited access to all platform capabilities

    <Tip>
      Start with the Free tier to explore the platform. You can upgrade anytime as your research needs grow.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete setup">
    Once you've selected your plan, you'll be redirected to the homepage and can start using ScholarXIV immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Search for research papers

ScholarXIV's powerful search engine helps you find relevant papers from the arXiv repository.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use the recommended search">
    When you first land on the homepage, ScholarXIV automatically loads papers based on a recommended keyword. Click the keyword to run a new random search:

    ```
    Recommended keyword "machine learning"
    ```

    This is a great way to discover interesting research across various topics.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Perform a custom search">
    Enter your search query in the search bar to find specific papers. The search supports:

    - **Keywords** - Search by topic, methodology, or concepts
    - **Authors** - Find papers by specific researchers
    - **Titles** - Search for papers by title

    Example searches:
    ```
    transformer architecture
    attention mechanisms
    computer vision
    natural language processing
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Refine with feed controls">
    Click the settings icon (⚙️) to access advanced filtering options:

    **Sort by:**
    - Relevance (default)
    - Last Updated Date
    - Submitted Date

    **Sort order:**
    - Ascending
    - Descending

    **Pagination:**
    - Navigate between pages of results
    - Adjust the number of results per page (default: varies, adjustable with +/- controls)

    <Tip>
      Use "Sort by Last Updated Date" with "Descending" order to find the most recent papers in your field.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Browse search results">
    Each paper card displays:
    - Paper title
    - Authors
    - Abstract preview
    - Published date
    - Action buttons (like, bookmark, select for chat)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Start an AI chat session

ScholarXIV's AI assistant helps you understand and analyze research papers through natural conversation.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select papers for analysis">
    In your search results, you'll see a "Select All" button and individual selection options on each paper card.

    - Click individual papers to select specific ones
    - Use "Select All" to analyze all papers on the current page
    - Selected papers appear in your chat context

    <Note>
      You can select multiple papers to compare and analyze them together in a single conversation.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to the chat interface">
    Once you've selected papers, click the chat button to start a new conversation. You'll be taken to a dedicated chat page at:

    ```
    /chats/[chatid]
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask questions about your papers">
    Start asking questions about the selected papers. The AI assistant can help you:

    **Summarize content:**
    ```
    Can you summarize the main findings of these papers?
    ```

    **Compare approaches:**
    ```
    How do these papers differ in their methodology?
    ```

    **Explain concepts:**
    ```
    Explain the attention mechanism described in this paper
    ```

    **Extract information:**
    ```
    What datasets were used in these experiments?
    ```

    <Tip>
      The AI has context about all selected papers and can reference specific sections, compare findings, and synthesize information across multiple papers.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="View conversation history">
    All your chat sessions are saved and accessible from the chats page:

    ```
    /chats
    ```

    You can return to previous conversations anytime to continue your research or reference past analyses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Organize your research

Keep track of important papers with ScholarXIV's organizational features.

### Like and bookmark papers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Like papers">
    Click the like button on any paper card to add it to your liked papers. View all liked papers at:

    ```
    /liked_papers_page
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Bookmark papers">
    Click the bookmark button to save papers for later. Access your bookmarks at:

    ```
    /bookmarks_page
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Create collections

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to collections">
    Go to the collections page:

    ```
    /collections
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new collection">
    Organize papers into custom collections for different projects, topics, or research areas.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share collections">
    Share your collections with collaborators using the share feature. Shared collections are accessible at:

    ```
    /collections/share/[id]
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Read and download papers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a paper">
    Click on any paper title to view it in the in-browser PDF viewer. Papers can be read directly without downloading.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download PDFs">
    Use the download button on any paper card to save the PDF to your local device for offline reading.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Join the discussion

Engage with the research community through ScholarXIV's comment system.

<Steps>
  <Step title="View paper discussions">
    Navigate to the comments page for any paper:

    ```
    /comments/papers/[paperid]
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your insights">
    Leave comments, ask questions, or share your perspective on papers. See what other researchers are discussing and join the conversation.
  </Step>
</Steps>
