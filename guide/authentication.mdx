ScholarXIV offers multiple authentication options to access the platform. You can sign in using your existing accounts from popular providers.

## Available Authentication Providers

ScholarXIV supports the following authentication methods:

- **Google** - Sign in with your Google account
- **GitHub** - Sign in with your GitHub account
- **Twitter** - Sign in with your Twitter/X account
- **Hugging Face** - Sign in with your Hugging Face account

<Note>
All authentication is handled securely through OAuth 2.0. ScholarXIV does not store your passwords.
</Note>

## Signing In

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the sign-in page">
    Go to the [ScholarXIV sign-in page](https://scholarxiv.com/auth/sign_in) or click "Sign In" from the landing page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose your authentication provider">
    Select one of the available authentication providers:
    - Google
    - GitHub
    - Twitter
    - Hugging Face
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Authorize the application">
    You'll be redirected to your chosen provider's authorization page. Sign in to your account and grant ScholarXIV the necessary permissions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete account setup">
    After successful authentication, you'll be redirected to the checkout page to select your subscription plan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
First-time users will automatically have a Polar customer account created for subscription management.
</Info>

## Session Management

Your ScholarXIV session is designed for convenience and security:

- **Session Duration**: 7 days
- **Auto-Refresh**: Sessions are refreshed every 24 hours when you're active
- **Automatic Sign-Out**: You'll be signed out after 7 days of inactivity

## Account Information

Once signed in, you can view your account information from the profile menu:

1. Click on your profile avatar in the sidebar
2. Your account details are displayed:
   - Profile picture
   - Display name
   - Email address
   - Current subscription plan

## Signing Out

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the profile menu">
    Click on your profile avatar in the sidebar to open the dropdown menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Logout">
    Click the "Logout" option at the bottom of the menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm sign-out">
    You'll be automatically signed out and redirected to the landing page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Signing out will end your current session. You'll need to sign in again to access your research, bookmarks, and other features.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="I can't sign in with my provider">
  If you're having trouble signing in:
  
  1. **Check your browser settings** - Ensure cookies and JavaScript are enabled
  2. **Clear browser cache** - Sometimes cached data can interfere with authentication
  3. **Try a different provider** - If one provider isn't working, try signing in with another
  4. **Check provider status** - Verify that your authentication provider (Google, GitHub, etc.) is operational
  5. **Disable browser extensions** - Some ad blockers or privacy extensions may interfere with OAuth
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="My session keeps expiring">
  If your session expires frequently:
  
  - Sessions last 7 days but require activity every 24 hours to refresh
  - Check if your browser is set to clear cookies on close
  - Ensure you're not in private/incognito mode
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I link multiple authentication providers?">
  Currently, each ScholarXIV account is tied to a single authentication provider. If you want to switch providers, you'll need to create a new account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What permissions does ScholarXIV request?">
  ScholarXIV requests minimal permissions from authentication providers:
  
  - **Google**: Basic profile information and email address
  - **GitHub**: Basic profile information and email address
  - **Twitter**: Basic profile information and email address
  - **Hugging Face**: Basic profile information and email address
  
  We do not request access to your repositories, posts, or other private data.
</Accordion>
